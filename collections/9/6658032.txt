This is why glaucoma is sometimes called the “silent thief of sight.”. Vision loss from glaucoma can make things look blurry around the edges. A type of glaucoma called “angle-closure” or “narrow-angle” glaucoma can cause sudden symptoms, including: 1  Severe eye pain. 2  Headache. 3  Nausea and vomiting. 4  Halos around lights. 5  Vision loss, including sudden loss of side (peripheral) vision.
