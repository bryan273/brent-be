Hemoglobin is also found outside red blood cells and their progenitor lines. Other cells that contain hemoglobin include the A9 dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra, macrophages, alveolar cells, and mesangial cells in the kidney. Hemoglobin S (α 2 β S 2) – A variant form of hemoglobin found in people with sickle cell disease. 2  There is a variation in the β-chain gene, causing a change in the properties of hemoglobin, which results in sickling of red blood cells.
