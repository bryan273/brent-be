The most common metabolic causes of intermittent ataxia in children are associated with hyperammonemia. Ornithine transcarbamylase deficiency (OTC) classically presents in the first 3 days of life.ysosomal storage diseases can also present with ataxia. Metachromatic leukodystrophy (MLD) is the most common of these disorders. The enzyme deficiency causes demyelination of the peripheral and central nervous system (CNS). The age of onset is variable, although early childhood is the most common age of onset.
