The pes anserinus (or pes anserine tendon) is a combined tendon of three muscles (Gracilis, Sartorius & Semitendinosus). It is found on the inside of the knee joint and passes slightly anteriorly to attach to the inner, front (anteromedial) Tibia (shin bone).ymptoms. The symptoms of pes anserinus tendonitis start with a gradually worsening pain on the inner knee, below the knee cap level. Initially the pain may ease as you warm up into your run, As the condition progresses, the pain will persist and often worsen.
