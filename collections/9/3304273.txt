An adult deer tick. (Wiki). An earlier version of this story indicated that certain counties in Alabama had been suffering from an epidemic, or suddenly increased number of cases of Lyme disease. The disease is 'endemic' to these counties,  which indicates a constant presence in an area. Calhoun, Chambers, Jefferson, Mobile, Shelby, Russell and Tuscaloosa counties have been declared areas endemic to Lyme disease, according to the Alabama Department of Public Health. Lyme disease is caused by certain ticks and can cause extreme health problems.
