Tests to diagnose acid reflux (GERD) include and upper GI series (X-rays of the esophagus, stomach, and upper part of the intestine), an upper GI endoscopy, esophageal manometry, or a 24-hour pH probe study. Home remedies for acid reflux include changes in lifestyle, diet, and habits. Treatment of acid reflux includes over-the-counter (OTC) medications including antacids and H2-blockers; prescription medications such as proton pump inhibitors, coating agents, and promotility agents; and in severe cases, surgery.
