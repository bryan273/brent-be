Medullary cavity. From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia. The medullary cavity (medulla, innermost part) is the central cavity of bone shafts where red bone marrow and/or yellow bone marrow (adipose tissue) is stored; hence, the medullary cavity is also known as the marrow cavity.owever, the medullary cavity is the area inside any bone (long, flat, etc.) that holds the bone marrow.
