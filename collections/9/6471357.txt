Education and experience required to become a Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetist (CRNA) include: A Bachelor’s of Science in Nursing (BSN) or other appropriate baccalaureate degree. A current license as a registered nurse.At least one year’s experience in an acute care nursing setting.Graduation from an accredited graduate school of nurse anesthesia. These educational programs range from 24-36 months, depending upon university requirements, and offer a master’s degree.n 1952, AANA established a mechanism for accreditation of nurse anesthesia educational programs that has been recognized by the U.S. Department of Education since 1955. In 1990, the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services published findings indicating a growing need for additional nurse anesthetists.
