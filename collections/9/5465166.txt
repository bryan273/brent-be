Women may be especially prone to UTIs because they have shorter urethras, which allow bacteria quick access to the bladder. Having sex can introduce bacteria into the urinary tract, too. Symptoms of UTIs. To identify a UTI, keep an eye out for the following symptoms: 1  A burning feeling when you urinate. 2  A frequent or intense urge to urinate, even though little comes out when you do. 3  Pain or pressure in your back or lower abdomen. 4  Cloudy, dark, bloody, or strange-smelling urine. 5  Feeling tired or shaky.
