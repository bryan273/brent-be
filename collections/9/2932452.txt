Too much vitamin A may be harmful to a developing baby in the first three months of pregnancy. As a result, the Department of Health advises all pregnant women and women planning a pregnancy to avoid foods or supplements which contain high levels of vitamin A such as liver, liver sausage, liver pate pâté and cod liver. oilaw shell fish. Raw shell fish sometimes contains harmful bacteria or viruses that can cause food poisoning. Unpasteurised milk. All unpasteurised milk including cows', goats' and sheep's milk should be avoided.
