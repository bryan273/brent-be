Many times when you have Leukocytosis you may or may not have any symptoms. If you do have any symptoms they are usually caused by the disease that is causing the increase in your white blood cell production. Some of the common symptoms you could have include: 1  Having a high fever. 2  Bruising or bleeding. 3  Feeling tired, sick, or weak. 4  Feeling faint, sweaty, or dizzy. 5  Having tingling or pain in your legs, abdomen, or arms. 6  Having trouble breathing. 7  Having trouble seeing or thinking. 8  Loss of appetite.
