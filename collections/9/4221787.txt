Type 1 diabetes can affect major organs in your body, including heart, blood vessels, nerves, eyes and kidneys.Keeping your blood sugar level close to normal most of the time can dramatically reduce the risk of many complications. Long-term complications of type 1 diabetes develop gradually, over decades. Kidney damage (nephropathy). 2  The kidneys contain millions of tiny blood vessel clusters that filter waste from your blood. 3  Diabetes can damage this delicate filtering system. 4  Severe damage can lead to kidney failure or irreversible end-stage kidney disease, which requires dialysis or a kidney transplant.
