except. You use except to introduce the only thing, person, or group that your main statement does not apply to. 1. used with a noun phrase. You usually use except in front of a noun phrase. Anything, except water, is likely to block a sink.All the boys except Peter started to laugh.You can use except in front of object pronouns such as me, him, or her, or in front of reflexive pronouns such as himself or herself.. 'accept'. Accept is a verb. If someone offers you something and you accept it, you agree to take it. I never accept presents from clients. See accept. 2. 'except'. Except is a preposition or conjunction. You use it to show that a statement does not include a particular thing or person.
