Freedom of Belief. The First Amendment does not expressly speak in terms of liberty to hold such beliefs as one chooses, but in both the religion and the expression clauses, it is clear, liberty of belief is the foundation of the liberty to practice what religion one chooses and to express oneself as one chooses.Footnote 174] Id. at 594. Justice Stone alone dissented, arguing that the First Amendment religion and speech clauses forbade coercion of ''these children to express a sentiment which, as they interpret it, they do not entertain, and which violates their deepest religious convictions.'' Id. at 601.
