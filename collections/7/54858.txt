Though Hemingray was best-known for its telegraph insulators, the company produced many other glass items including bottles, fruit jars, pressed glass dishes, tumblers, battery jars, fishbowls, lantern globes, and oil lamps.n 1933, the company was sold to the Owens-Illinois Glass Company but production remained in Muncie under the Hemingray name. The main plant in Muncie shut down in 1966 and insulator production ceased.
