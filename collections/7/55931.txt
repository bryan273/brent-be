noun. A rhetorical figure in which incongruous or contradictory terms are combined, as in a deafening silence and a mournful optimist. Greek oxumōron, an expression that is witty because paradoxical, from neuter of oxumōros, pointedly foolish: oxus, sharp, keen; see oxygen + mōros, dull, foolish. Origin of oxymoron. Related Forms: 1  ox′y·mo·ron′ic.
