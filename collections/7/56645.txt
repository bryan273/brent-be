Fry-Jefferson map showing Virginia-North Carolina boundary, from Roanoke River to Dan River (showing Peters Creek where 1749 survey started) Virginia-North Carolina boundary, as extended in 1749 west to a tributary of the Holston River near modern-day Damascus, Virginia. Source: Library of Congress, A map of the most inhabited part of Virginia containing the whole province of Maryland with part of Pensilvania, New Jersey and North Carolina.
