Gamekeeper's Thumb. Gamekeeper’s thumb is a repetitive stress injury of the ulnar collateral ligament—tough fibrous tissue that connects the bones at the base of the thumb. Over time, overuse leads to the loosening of the ulnar ligament.Gamekeeper’s thumb most often occurs as a result of chronic overuse in a sport such as skiing in which gripping of the pole exerts repeated stress on the thumb.ver time, overuse leads to the loosening of the ulnar ligament.
