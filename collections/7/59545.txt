[ɪˈvan pʲɪˈtrovʲɪtɕ ˈpavləf]. ; 26 September [ O.S. 14 September] 1849 – 27 February 1936) was a Russian physiologist known primarily for his work in classical conditioning. From his childhood days Pavlov demonstrated intellectual brilliance along with an unusual energy which he named the instinct for research.
