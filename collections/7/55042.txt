When could is used as the past tense of can, it refers to an ability that a person generally had in the past or to something that was generally possible in the past (When I was younger, I could run for miles, or It used to be you could buy lunch for a dollar.).ike can, could can be used of possibility as well, but the connotation is slightly different. In the sentence We can have as many as ten people for dinner tonight, can is used to show ability (We are able to have as many as ten people for dinner tonight; “We have enough food/space for ten people.”).
