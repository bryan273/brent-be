Why Do Babies’ Eyes Change Color? When we talk about baby eye color, we’re referring to the color of the iris—the part of the eye behind the cornea that controls the size of the pupil to let light in. There are two reasons why baby eye color changes: genetics and melanin. Genetics. The genes babies inherit from both parents play a role in determining their newborn eye color. In fact, experts say there are approximately 15 genes that are responsible for baby eye color, but two—OCA2 and HERC2—are the most predominant.
