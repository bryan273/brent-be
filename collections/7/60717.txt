We often use except and except for as prepositions to mean ‘ not including’ or ‘excluding’. They are followed by a noun or noun phrase or a wh-clause. Both except and except for are correct after a noun:I like all fruit except (for) oranges.(excluding oranges). Except for Louisa, who’s away in Berlin this weekend, we’ll all be at the party.
