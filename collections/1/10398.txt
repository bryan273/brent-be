The Reaction Distance is the distance the car travels in the three-quarters of a second that it takes for a driver to react to a problem and apply the brakes. The Braking Distance is the distance traveled from the time the brakes are first applied to the time the car comes to a complete stop. The Stopping Distance is the total distance it takes to stop the vehicle, from the moment the driver sees a problem to the time the car is stopped completely. = Stopping Distance.
