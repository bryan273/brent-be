Slipstream fiction turns tales that mesh fantasy and reality. The latest addition to the genre-crossing approach is ‘Get in Trouble,’ a short-story collection by Kelly Link, out this week. Updated Feb. 4, 2015 1:25 p.m. Local news is different in Kelly Link’s world. “A woman had given birth to half a dozen rabbits,” says a radio announcer in one of Ms. Link’s short stories. Ms. Link’s weaving of the real and unreal is part of a fast-growing strain of fiction some call slipstream.
