Till or dig up the area where carrots will be planted. Make sure the soil is tilled up to soften and aerate the ground to make it easier to grow carrots long and straight. Fertilize the soil with one cup of 10-20-10 for every 10 feet of row you plant.You can use a rake to mix the soil and fertilizer.hen growing carrots in the garden, you will wait for your carrot plants to start to show. When the plants are four inches high, you can thin the plants to two inches apart. You may find that some of the carrots are actually large enough to eat.
