a measure of the packed cell volume of red cells, expressed as a percentage of the total blood volume. The normal range is between 43% and 49% in men and between 37% and 43% in women.Also spelled haematocrit.See also complete blood count, differential white blood cell count.ematocrit (hē·maˑ·t ·krit) , n the number of red blood cells in a sample expressed as a percentage of the total blood volume. The normal hematocrit range is between 43% to 49% in men and 37% to 43% in women.
