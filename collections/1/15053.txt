Houston, the nation's fourth largest city in terms of population, is the 51st most dense of the 100 most populous cities, registering about 3,500 people per square mile. A story in the October issue of Governing explores how the city became the country's most sprawling major metro area. READ: More urban news. Further north, Dallas is just about as densely populated and is the ninth most populous city. Similar cities include Phoenix (sixth most populous nationally) with a population density of 2,798 people/sq. mile and San Antonio (seventh most populous) with 2,880 people/sq. mile.
