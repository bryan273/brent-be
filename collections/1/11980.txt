Articulating bone(s). There are at least 2 bones forming each joint. An example of a simple synovial joint is the 3rd metacarpophalangeal (MCP) joint, which is formed by two articulating bones, the 3rd metacarpal and the 3rd proximal phalanx.ynovial joints are freely movable joints. A formal medical term is  diarthroses  but synovial joints are commonly described as simply  movable  or  freely movable . Most of the main joints of the appendicular skeleton (including the arms and hands, and the legs and feet) are synovial joints.
