PROBLEMS IN TRANSITION. 1  Symptoms of greater than 2 hours' duration. 2  Worsening distress. 3  Congenital anomalies. 4  Abnormal muscle tone. 5  Central cyanosis. 6  Apnea in a near-term or term infant. 7  Moderate-to-severe respiratory distress: grunting, flaring, marked retractions and need for supplemental oxygen beyond 2 hours of age.
