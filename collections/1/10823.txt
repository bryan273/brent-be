However, having too much fiber in one's diet can cause problems. When the intake of fiber is too high, it can replace other energy and nutrients that you need in your diet. Some insoluble fibers bind certain minerals, including calcium, magnesium, phosphorous, and iron. Controlling your appetite/weight. 2  Foods that contain fiber are typically low in fat, energy-dense, take more time to chew, keep you full for longer, and block some of the digestion of fats and proteins. 3  Preventing cancer. 4  Fiber consumption may lower the risk for colorectal cancer, but the evidence is not yet conclusive.
