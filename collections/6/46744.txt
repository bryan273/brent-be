However, this small-diameter pipe is usually used only in gathering and distribution systems, although some is used for control-line or gauge-line purposes. Mainline pipes, the principal pipeline in a given system, are usually between 16 and 48 inches in diameter. Lateral pipelines, which deliver natural gas to or from the mainline, are typically between 6 and 16 inches in diameter. Most major interstate pipelines are between 24 and 36 inches in diameter.
