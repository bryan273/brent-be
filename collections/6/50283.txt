Reporting requirements apply to all currency, regardless of where it's from. NA/PhotoObjects.net/Getty Images. Federal laws designed to prevent money laundering require that transactions be reported to the government if they involve large amounts of currency -- that is, cash and coins.anks can get an exemption from the reporting requirements for their business customers who do a lot of sales in cash, such as supermarkets or restaurants, and therefore frequently deposit more than $10,000. However, federal regulations push the reporting requirements down the line to those businesses.
