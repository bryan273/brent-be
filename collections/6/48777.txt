Side effects from methocarbamol may be worse if you have kidney or liver disease. You may also be at higher risk for side effects if you're 65 or older. Combining methocarbamol with alcohol or another drug that depresses your central nervous system can be dangerous. Side effects of methocarbamol include: Drowsiness. Nausea and vomiting. Fever. Change in urine color. Swelling under the skin. Headache. Flushing.
