Pseudostratified Columnar Epithelium. Tissue composed of a single layer of elongated cells that appear to be layered because the nuclei are at two or more levels in the row of aligned cells, even though all cells touch the basement membrane (p. 94).imple Columnar Epithelium. Tissue composed of a single layer of elongated cells whose nuclei are usually located at about the same level, near the basement membrane; cells may be ciliated or nonciliated (p. 94).
