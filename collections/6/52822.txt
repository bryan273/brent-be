The Paper’s Tooth. In watercolor terms, when we are talking about the paper’s tooth we are actually talking about the texture or smoothness of the paper’s surface. This is where our terms hot and cold pressed originate from. In simple terms, Cold Pressed paper means the paper was designed with a “bumpy texture or a great deal of tooth. An easy way to remember this is by thinking about yourself bing cold. When you are cold you tend to develop goosebumps.
