Cleveland was the son of Richard Falley Cleveland, a Presbyterian minister who died when Grover was 16, and Ann Neal. He had five sisters and three brothers. On June 2, 1886, Cleveland married Frances Folsom in a ceremony in the White House. He was 49 and she was 21. Together they had three daughters and two sons. His daughter Esther was the only President's child born in the White House. Cleveland was alleged to have a child by a premarital affair with Maria Halpin. He was unsure of the child's paternity but accepted responsibility. Grover Cleveland's Career Before the Presidency: Cleveland went into law practice and became an active member of the Democratic Party in New York. He became Sheriff of Erie County, New York from 1871-73.
