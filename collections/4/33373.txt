Diagram showing the development of different blood cells from haematopoietic stem cell to mature cells. Haematopoiesis (from Greek αἷμα, blood and ποιεῖν to make; also hematopoiesis in American English; sometimes also haemopoiesis or hemopoiesis) is the formation of blood cellular components.ll cellular blood components are derived from haematopoietic stem cells. In a healthy adult person, approximately 10 11 –10 12 new blood cells are produced daily in order to maintain steady state levels in the peripheral circulation.
