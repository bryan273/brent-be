Katla, subglacial volcano, southern Iceland, located underneath Mýrdalsjökull (Mýrdals Glacier). Katla’s summit sits at 4,961 feet (1,512 metres) above sea level, and its oval caldera is about 6 miles (10 km) across at its widest. Katla is one of Iceland’s most active volcanoes, having erupted at least 20 times since the year 1100. Some of these eruptions have been preceded by or occurred simultaneously with eruptions of Eyjafjallajökull volcano, located some 15.5 miles (25 km) to the west. Volcanoes and glaciers of Iceland.
