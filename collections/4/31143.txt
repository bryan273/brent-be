The thermosphere is the highest section of the Earth's atmosphere. It starts about 53 miles above sea level and extends to between 311 to 621 miles.The exact extent of the thermosphere varies, as it swells and contracts based on the current level of solar activity.he source of the thermosphere's heat is radiation emitted by the sun. The thermosphere absorbs much of the radiation that Earth receives from the sun, leaving only a fraction to actually reach the surface.
