Some light planes are able to climb to 25,000 or 30,000 feet and fly 250-350 MPH. Passenger jets typically fly at 25,000 to 40,000 feet when on duty, but some can actually fly much higher. The Boeing 747-400 is capable of climbing to 55,000 feet. These airplanes have maximum cruise speeds in the range of 475 to 625 MPH.
