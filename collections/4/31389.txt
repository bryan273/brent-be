Slavery had been abolished by the Thirteenth Amendment, but the white people of the South were determined to keep the Negro in his place, socially, politically, and economically. This was done by means of the notorious Black Codes, passed by several of the state legislatures. Northerners regarded these codes as a revival of slavery in disguise.
