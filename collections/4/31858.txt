The name ABBA was an acronym derived from the group's members, Benny Anderson and Bjorn Ulvaeus, as well as Agnetha Faltskog, and Anni-Frid Lyngstad. The name was first used in the summer of 1972. ABBA last performed together in public in December of 1982 and its last singles were released in 1983. ABBA is one of the most successful pop acts of all time, and is the second-best selling band behind only The Beatles. Universal Music certified in 2010 that ABBA has sold over 375,000,000 records and still sells about 5,000,000 each year.
