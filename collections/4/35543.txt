Herniated disc pain may be experienced throughout the body, not merely in the upper or lower back. Depending on the location and severity of the disc herniation, symptoms can range from chronic neck and back discomfort to severe pain that travels to the legs or arms, usually on one side of the body.ciatica is a set of symptoms that might include herniated disc pain and the sensation of sharp pain or “pins and needles” in the buttocks, legs and feet. A pinched nerve caused by a herniated disc in the cervical spine (neck) might be the cause of a burning sensation in the arms or soreness in the shoulders.
