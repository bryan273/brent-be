THE FOXTROT & QUICKSTEP. In the early days of the Twentieth Century, during the Ragtime Era, a whole flock of animal dances were briefly popular. There was the Squirrel, in which dancers assumed closed position and took small, mincing steps: 1&, 2&, 3&, hold, feet closed, knees soft, and up on the toes; or quick, quick, quick, quick; slow, slow.
