Many underlying conditions may trigger groin pain in women, some more serious than the other. Recurrent pain and severe pain that does not subside for a longer period of time require immediate evaluation. Common Causes of Groin Pain in Women. Women may feel groin pain after an injury induced by falling or getting hit. Female athletes may strain some of the muscles in the area, triggering the pain subsequently. One of the most common causes of groin pain in women is pregnancy. This is not hard to realize and understand since women in this period go through many physical changes, especially in the pelvic area. Other Conditions Related To Groin Pain. Groin pain may also be triggered by numerous infections caused either by viruses or bacteria in the groin area or even the urinary tract.
