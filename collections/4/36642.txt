Whether you choose to take a day hike or head into the backcountry for an extended stay, an abundance of trails are available to all who visit Sandpoint. The Forest Service, Idaho Department of Lands, and private citizens maintain an ever expanding trails system. The Sandpoint Ranger District maintains over 275 miles of trails in the National Forest. Many trailheads provide access to prime fishing, huckleberry picking and even an occasional morel mushroom or two.
