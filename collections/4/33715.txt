Etymology[edit] From Old Irish ithid ‎(“eats, bites, devours; grazes”), from Proto-Celtic *ɸit-e/o-, from Proto-Indo-European *peyt-. The future stem is from Old Irish ·íss, from Proto-Celtic *ɸi-ɸit-sā-. The occasional Munster past tense form duaidh is from Old Irish ·dúaid (deuterotonic do·fúaid), from dí- + fo- + Proto-Celtic *ed-, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁ed-.
