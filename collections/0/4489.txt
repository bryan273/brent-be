Exercise, of course, will raise your heart rate above 100, and that’s good (as long as you don’t exceed your target heart rate). But a resting pulse that suddenly rises to 100 beat per minute or more can be a sign of tachycardia – a more serious disturbance in the rhythm of the heart.
