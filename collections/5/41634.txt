Outline This web page explores the Learned Helplessness theory of depression developed by Martin Seligman in the 1970s. It begins by outlining the symptoms that any theory of depression must account for.he central axiom of the theory-that helplessness is a cognitive state in humans and animals is emphasized, before comparing helplessness in animals with human depression. The Motor Activation Deficit hypothesis represents a major challenge to Learned Helplessness theory.
