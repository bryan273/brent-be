St. Augustine grass (Stenotaphrum secundatum) prefers the warm, humid climate and grows in a wide variety of soils. Hardy in U.S. Department of Agriculture hardiness zones 8 through 11, St. Augustine grass is prized as a green to blue-green turf that grows densely and establishes rapidly.hinch Bugs. Chinch bugs are a major pest of St. Augustine grass, producing several generations per year. Immature chinch bugs are tiny, bright red insects with a white band over their back. The winged adults are black and about 1/5 inch long.
