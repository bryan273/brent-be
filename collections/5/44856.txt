For as long as there have been records, St. Augustine grass has been reported as a seashore pioneer along the Atlantic coasts of Africa and the Americas. Prior to 1800, the species was reported in Uruguay, Brazil, Nigeria, Sierra Leone, the West Indies, Bermuda and South Carolina.his species is called St. Augustine grass and sometimes carpetgrass in the Southeastern United States and in California, crabgrass in Bermuda and the West Indies, gramillon in Argentina, wiregrass in St. Helena and buffalograss in Australia and the South Pacific.
