ABC/Kevin Winter, Getty. Then: Brady had appeared in several episodes of the original 'Whose Line,' before being cast as a regular on its American counterpart. Through his charismatic and song-stuffed performances, he became the show's breakout star and earned his own talk show, 'The Wayne Brady Show,' in 2002.Whose Line' followed two years later, then in 2007 Carey took to hosting a real game show, taking over for Bob Barker on the price-guessing game 'The Price Is Right.' In 2011, a slimmed down Carey called much of the 'Whose Line' gang back together for the short-lived comedy series 'Drew Carey's Improv-A-Ganza.'.
