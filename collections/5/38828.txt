In substitutive chemical nomenclature, their names are formed by adding '-oyl' as a suffix to the name of the parent compound; ethanoyl chloride, CH 3 COCl, is an example. The terms acyl and aroyl halides refer to aliphatic or aromatic derivatives, respectively.n substitutive chemical nomenclature, their names are formed by adding '-oyl' as a suffix to the name of the parent compound; ethanoyl chloride, CH 3 COCl, is an example.
