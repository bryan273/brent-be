Except for the second movement all other movements are in sonata-allegro form, an arrangement which will be followed again only in the 4th Symphony. 2. Symphony No.2 in D Major, Op.36 - composed in 1802 - third movement: Scherzo-D Major; Allegro - Song and Trio Design. First performance 1803. This movement, and the related movements in all the symphonies is not called a menuetto, but a scherzo. In fact, the only symphonies which use the name menuetto are the First Symphony and The Eighth which calls for a Tempo di Menuetto.
