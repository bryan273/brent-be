Taiga regions are also known as boreal forest zones, and these areas are located in the western region that is home to pines, shrubs, grass and light soil. Podzol is the most common soil type in taiga areas, and it is acidic with a light soil color. The mixed forests are located in warmer areas, and spruce and oak are the most common trees. Other plant life, such as elm, ash, pine and maple, exist in mixed zones.
