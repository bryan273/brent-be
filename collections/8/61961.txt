Maybe you’re wondering if you’re learning the skills you need to actually succeed in this career. That’s OK because HIT skills are pretty job-specific: Medical coding and knowledge of medical terminology and medical records all fall into the scope of specialized skills you’ll need. But that’s not where the list ends if you want to be a successful health information technician. An analysis of nearly 11,000 online job postings* identified the top 10 skills employers are looking for in their HIT candidates. Whether you code a patient’s medical procedure, analyze data in Excel or write a memo to your boss, these are all skills you’ll need to make it in the real world.
