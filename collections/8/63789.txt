Implications for Overtime Pay. Although there may not be laws requiring time off between shifts, you may be entitled to overtime if the shifts fall within certain time periods. Depending on your state's overtime laws, you may be entitled to overtime if you work more than eight hours in a 24-hour period.For example, if you work a 2 a.m.-10:30 a.m. shift, and you are scheduled to return at 11 p.m. that same evening, your hour between 11 p.m. and midnight might be counted as overtime.here are certain industry exceptions in which there are regulations regarding the number of hours required between shifts for public safety reasons. Truck and bus drivers must generally take a minimum of eight hours off between shifts, and their shift hours are often capped.
