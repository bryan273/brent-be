Cardiac causes of syncope often are unprovoked and are more likely to present in the emergency department. Sudden death in young adults with syncope is often the result of arrhythmias. Orthostatic hypotension can be caused by autonomic dysfunction, medications, or volume depletion resulting in syncope. It is rare in patients younger than 40 years, and is commonly seen in older patients with comorbid conditions.1,6.
