Fever is the temporary increase in the body's temperature in response to a disease or illness. A child has a fever when the temperature is at or above one of these levels: 1  100.4°F (38°C) measured in the bottom (rectally) 2  99.5°F (37.5°C) measured in the mouth (orally)  99°F (37.2°C) measured under the arm (axillary)
