Cell phones are causing more and more car crashes. An man works his phone as he drives through traffic in Dallas, Tuesday, Feb. 26, 2013. More than a quarter of all car crashes in America are likely caused by cell phone use, a study by the Nation Safety Council has shown. According to the NSC, the estimate for crashes caused by texting rose to at least 6% in 2013. Additionally, 21% of crashes in 2013 were related to the use of handheld or hands-free phones.
