Several other entities that involve the peripatellar fat may be encountered when evaluating MR of the knee. Of these, one of the most common is quadriceps/suprapatellar fat pad impingement syndrome. A study by Roth, et al.10 demonstrates mass effect from the quadriceps fat pad on the suprapatellar recess (defined as a posterior convex border) in 12% of 92 consecutive knee MRI examinations.
