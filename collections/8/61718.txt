Chest X-Ray. Guide. A chest X-ray is a picture of the chest that shows your heart, lungs, airway, blood vessels, and lymph nodes. A chest X-ray also shows the bones of your spine and chest, including your breastbone, your ribs, your collarbone, and the upper part of your spine.A chest X-ray is the most common imaging test or X-ray used to find problems inside the chest. chest X-ray is done to: 1  Help find the cause of common symptoms such as a cough, shortness of breath, or chest pain.
