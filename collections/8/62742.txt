In turn the name means “glory of Zeus, glory to Zeus”. The name has spread from the Latin “Dĭŏclētĭānus” (Ancient Greek: Dioklētiānós ‎”Διοκλητιᾱνός”). Diocletian was a Roman Emperor from 284 to 305. Born to a family of low status in the Roman province of Dalmatia, Diocletian rose through the ranks of the military to become cavalry commander to the Emperor Carus.
