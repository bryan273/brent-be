A: Bipolar disorder is often managed with medications called mood stabilizers. Mood stabilizers help even out the mood swings that occur with bipolar disorder. Medications that are used to stabilize mood include lithium (Eskalith, Lithobid), Depakote (divalproex sodium), and Tegretol (carbamazepine).: Lithium is commonly used to mania episodes in people who suffer from manic-depressive disorder. Common side effects associated with Lithium can include hair loss, mild hand tremor, weakness, lack of coordination, mild nausea, vomiting, lack of appetite, stomach pain, and itchy skin.
