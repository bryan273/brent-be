Best Answer: Proteins are the most abunant class of macromolecules in the animal cell. Other macromolecules include the nucleic acids (DNA and RNA), lipids, polysaccharides, and lipopolysaccharides. However, it is cellulose the most abundant macromolecule in all living cells, if we consider plant cells, too. Cellulose makes most of the wall of plant cells and it is essential for their survival.
