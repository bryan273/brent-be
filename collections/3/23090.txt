Super-Delegates In 2008. In 2008, a candidate needs 2,025 votes to win the Democratic presidential nomination. There are about 850 super-delegates. Roll Call keeps a list of Congressional endorsements, or see this list at CNN and this one at Democratic Convention Watch. super-delegate is a leader in the National Democratic Party who has a vote at the national convention; they not selected by state party members. A candidate needs 2,025 delegates for the nomination.
