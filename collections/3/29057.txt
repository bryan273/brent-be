Thus, from a safety standpoint, butter left at room temperature is unlikely to do any harm. But there is one other concern that can’t be ignored: rancidity. If you’ve ever had a whiff of butter that’s been left out in the open for too long, you’ll know it well. “Off” flavors develop in butter when it’s exposed to oxygen, which does two things. First, it breaks down fat molecules, producing free fatty acids that have an unpleasant aroma and flavor.
