Six seconds. That’s how much time recruiters usually take to look at your resume. Your stellar academic record and long list of professional accomplishments, all in six seconds.In a 2012 study, researchers recorded their subjects’ eyeball movements, what they zeroed in on, and for how long.f they’re only spending six seconds on a resume, unless their reading rate is 6000 wpm with 90%+ retention, they’re not likely to retain enough information to make the scan worthwhile. If all they’re looking at is titles and education, they don’t know much more after “reading” the resume than they did before.
