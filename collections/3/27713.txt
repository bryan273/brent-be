Fat-soluble Vitamins. A, D, E and K vitamins are fat-soluble due to their ability to dissipate in fat. In contrast to water-soluble vitamins, these types of vitamins do not require frequent replenishments.Fat-soluble vitamins absorb dietary fat in the small intestines with a tendency to accumulate the excess amounts in the liver and fatty tissues.n contrast to water-soluble vitamins, these types of vitamins do not require frequent replenishments.
