Amnesty is defined as: A pardon extended by the government to a group or class of persons, usually for a political offense; the act of a sovereign power officially forgiving certain classes of persons who are subject to trial but have not yet been convicted. It includes more than pardon, inasmuch as it obliterates all legal remembrance of the offense. Amnesty is more and more used to express freedom and the time when prisoners can go free.
