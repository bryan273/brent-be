While it was an unsuccessful time for Kefauver, it was much more so for the primary system in general. Voter participation more than doubled in 1952 to 12.7 million (up from 4.8 million in 1948) (Davis 17). State party bosses still had power, but the public was gaining an increasing interest in the primary process. A Time for Reform
