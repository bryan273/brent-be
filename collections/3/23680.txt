But what about when you pull out that container of mashed potatoes (or even that pint of ice cream you want for a midnight snack) and there’s a layer of dreaded freezer burn? We investigate what that icy crust is, when the food is still safe to eat and how to avoid the problem in the future. What is Freezer Burn? Freezer burn occurs when your food dries out. Those ice crystals you see on your bulk pack of chicken or in your frozen leftovers is moisture that escaped from the food and turned into ice on the outside. It happens for one of two reasons: Either you didn’t store your food properly, or your food has just been in the freezer for a long time.
