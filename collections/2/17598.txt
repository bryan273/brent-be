Just like other muscles twitch (upper legs, back, arms, calves, etc.), the thumb is not exempt to this very common, benign phenomenon. The worry factor kicks in because when a thumb twitches, it can be seen in action. A person’s fixation and worry over a twitching thumb may evolve into imagining that the unit is weak, smaller than the other or visibly atrophied — possible signs of ALS — provided that these symptoms are real and not imagined, that is.
