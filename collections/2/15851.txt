1. Cost basis is generally the price you paid for your shares, adjusted for return of capital, certain corporate actions, and any sales charges or transaction fees. Cost basis is an important calculation used to determine gains and losses on any shares you sell in a taxable (non-retirement) account.. Not all cost basis methods listed will be available for every fund. The following cost basis methods may be available to you: 1  First In First Out (FIFO) A standing order to sell the oldest shares in the account first. 2  Last In First Out (LIFO) A standing order to sell the newest shares in an account first.
