T-cells are structured to help fight infection, while nerve cells need to be capable of transmitting and receiving the electrical impulses that characterize brain function. But one thing that all human cells have in common-from brain to eye to muscle to skin cells is that they contain DNA, the  blueprint of life . And each cell, regardless of its form or function, contains a complete set of DNA. This is the same DNA as is found in every other cell in a person's body.
