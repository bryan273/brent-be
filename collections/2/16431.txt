Word Origin and History for pork Expand n. c.1300 (early 13c. in surname Porkuiller), flesh of a pig as food, from Old French porc pig, swine, boar, and directly from Latin porcus pig, tame swine, from PIE *porko-young swine (cf. Umbrian purka; Old Church Slavonic prase young pig; Lithuanian parsas pig; and Old English fearh, Middle Dutch varken, both from Proto-Germanic *farhaz).
