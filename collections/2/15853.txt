It’s up to you to decipher what the 1099-B says. Brokers and fund companies send form 1099-B when you buy or sell an investment, like shares of a mutual fund or stock. The form is a record of those transactions.When you plug the information into a Schedule D, you and the IRS can figure out your capital gains or losses for the tax year.ach purchase is a separate transaction, a separate cost basis for you to track, and it’s own unique tax situation when sold.
