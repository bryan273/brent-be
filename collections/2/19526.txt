Paneer is made without rennet, by curdling hot milk with lemon or lime juice, vinegar, yoghurt or citric acid. Cubed paneer should be firm so that it doesn’t crumble easily, but not hard, and you should judge a paneer dish by how soft and fluffy the cheese is. Spicy paneer skewers/Prashad Cookbook: Indian Vegetarian Cuisine . Can you eat it fresh? Paneer is not always cubed and fried before use. It can be eaten fresh as it is, or crumbled, shaped, moulded and, occasionally, flavoured with cumin or mint. Homemade paneer is the best – it’s easy and quick to prepare, and you really will taste the difference.
