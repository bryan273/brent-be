If you're writing songs with others, as soon as you finish the song, agree with your collaborators as to how to split potential revenues. If you wait until after you have a deal or record the song, you could end up sorting out credits and payments with band members who have long since left the group.on't give up your copyright, is the cry often heard from musicians and songwriters. Yes, it's true that the music business is rife with tales of woe about songwriters like Richard Berry, who gave up his copyright for Louie, Louie for $750. (Berry eventually won a $2 million court judgment over the song.).
