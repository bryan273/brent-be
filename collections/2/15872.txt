The parathyroid glands (light pink) produce parathyroid hormone, which controls levels of calcium in the blood. The parathyroid glands are small pea-sized glands located in the neck just behind the butterfly-shaped thyroid gland. Most people have four parathyroid glands, with two parathyroid glands lying behind each 'wing' of the thyroid gland. What hormones do my parathyroid glands produce?
