Eye mucus is common in dogs — however, excessive discharge (especially when it's yellow or yellow-green) is a sure sign of trouble. Bacterial conjunctivitis, a cause of excessive eye mucus, can be highly contagious, so if you know your dog is infected, keep him away from other dogs and even human family members. By: jonkriz. Eye mucus in dogs is normal and can be caused by a number of things, from viruses to allergies. However, excessive discharge (especially when it’s yellow or yellow-green) could be a sign of an infection, glaucoma or other eye problems — even a brain or nerve injury. Learning more about the most common causes of excessive eye discharge will enable you to help your dog stay healthy.
