Savannah Holly. This holly grows quickly in full sun or partial shade on moist, acid soils. Growth is poor and foliage chlorotic on alkaline soil. Plants in full sun can grow a dense canopy, those in partial shade are more open. This plant is considered mostly allergy free and causes little or no allergy problems in most people. Trees attract cedar waxwings, mockingbirds, robbins and many other birds. A severe witches broom-like disease caused by Sphaeropsis fungus attacks and kills Ilex x attenuata cultivars in parts of Florida.
