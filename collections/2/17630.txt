Quartered in a far-away colony, Isaac Brock would emerge as one of Britain's most ablest and tragic figures. by Alain Gauthier. Of the military leaders who emerged from the brief and bitter conflict known as the War of 1812, few have been able to carve a reputation for themselves as bold, imaginative and inspiring leaders. Isaac Brock, however, personified these qualities in a military career that spanned three decades.
