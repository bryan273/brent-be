About 250,000 people gathered and listened to his immortal words: I have a dream that my four children will one day live in a nation where they will not be judged by the colour of their skin, but by the content of their character. A year later, in 1964, Martin Luther King, Jr. was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize. In his acceptance speech he said: I refuse to accept the view that mankind is so tragically bound to the starless midnight of racism and war that the bright daybreak of peace and brotherhood can never become a reality.
